# `fail-trust-anchor-mismatch.json`

**Expected verdict:** `fail`

**Exercises:** the trust-anchor pinning defense at the CMS signature-verification layer.

The vector's anchor claims `type: "tsa_freetsa"` (routing it through the TSA verification path), but the embedded token is signed by a local fixtures CA that was generated by `spec/tools/fixtures-ca.ts` and **never pinned anywhere**. The verifier's CMS signature verification therefore fails: the token's signer chain cannot be resolved against either of the two PEMs bundled in `src/trust-anchors/`.

The per-anchor check records `fail`, and the verdict is `fail`.

**Why this is the right defense surface.** The pin map in `src/trust-anchors/fingerprints.ts` is checked at load time against the bundled PEMs themselves — it catches the case where the *bundled* certs have been silently substituted. The `fail-trust-anchor-mismatch` vector tests a different case: a token signed by someone outside the pinned CA set. Both defenses compose.

**Generation:** `npm run fixtures:generate` synthesizes a throwaway CA + TSA cert under `spec/fixtures-trust-anchors/test-ca.pem`, signs a TimeStampToken with it, and stores the base64-encoded DER in this vector. The SHA-256(SKI) of that CA is recorded in `spec/fixtures-trust-anchors/fingerprints.ts` and asserted to have empty set-intersection with the production fingerprints by `scripts/check-fingerprints-disjoint.ts`.
